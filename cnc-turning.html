FAQ

CNC Turning — Frequently Asked Questions

What tolerance can you hold on CNC turning?

Our standard CNC turning tolerance is ±0.05mm (±0.002"). On critical diameters we hold ±0.01mm (±0.0004") with live-tooling lathes and in-process gauging. Concentricity and roundness are verified by CMM and roundness testers.

What is the maximum diameter and length you can turn?

Our CNC lathes turn parts up to 610 mm (24") in diameter and 1219 mm (48") in length as our regular capacity. Larger sizes are reviewed case-by-case — share your drawing and we’ll confirm capacity during DFM review across our 300+ machine fleet.

What materials can you CNC turn?

We turn aluminum (6061, 7075), stainless steel (303, 304, 316), carbon and alloy steels, titanium, brass, copper, and engineering plastics like PEEK and Delrin. Free-machining grades (303, 12L14) give the best surface finish and cycle time.

Do you offer Swiss-type turning for small precision parts?

Yes. Swiss-type (sliding headstock) turning is ideal for small-diameter, high-precision parts with tight tolerances — connector pins, shafts, and medical components. It delivers excellent concentricity on long, slender parts.

What is the lead time for CNC turned parts?

Prototypes ship in 3–7 business days; production runs typically 1–3 weeks depending on quantity and finishing. Every quote includes a confirmed lead time, reviewed by an engineer within 24 hours.
